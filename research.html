Research Overview

My research agenda focuses on Environmental Policy Stringency and Macro-Financial Dynamics. I extensively use Text Mining and Machine Learning to process large datasets (legislative texts, reports) to create novel economic indicators. I hold a qualification for the position of Maître de Conférences (Section 5 - Economic Sciences, 2026).

Publications & Working Papers

Do IMF Reports Affect Market Expectations? A Sentiment Analysis Approach H. Bennani (LEMNA), C. Couharde (EconomiX), & Y. Wallois (LEM) Review of World Economics, 2024 | HCERES A Ranked Journal

We analyze the impact of IMF communication on financial markets by extracting and analyzing sentiments from qualitative reports. Constitutes a database rating IMF sentiment towards 16 economies over 15 years using GARCH transformations and dynamic panel estimations.

A worldwide measure of environmental policy stringency: A text mining approach from legislations Y. Wallois (LEM) Working Paper (Submitted to Journal of Environmental Economics and Management)

Construction of a worldwide multidimensional index of environmental policy stringency. Based on text analysis of 70,000+ legislative texts covering 197 countries and 73 languages. Financed by LEM (2023) for the translation of 40,000 documents.

Climate-related Extreme Weather Events and Environmental Policy Stringency F. Huart (LEM) & Y. Wallois (LEM) Working Paper

Analysis of the impact of extreme events (droughts, heatwaves, floods) on environmental policies. Uses geocoded micro-economic data and non-linear probabilistic panel models.

Government spending, tax effort and fiscal multipliers: Evidence from EU members A. Abdelsalam (CLERSÉ) & Y. Wallois (LEM) Working Paper

Analysis of dynamic impacts of fiscal policies on household and firm behavior using non-linear structural VAR models.
