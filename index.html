Introduction

I am an Assistant Professor in Economics (Enseignant-Chercheur) at the FGES - Université Catholique de Lille and a researcher associated with the LITL (Laboratoire Interdisciplinaire des Transitions de Lille).

Holder of a PhD in Economics from the University of Lille (LEM, CNRS UMR 9221) since December 2024, my research lies at the intersection of Environmental Economics, Political Economy, and Data Science. I defend a thesis titled "Text mining and political economy: new applications to finance and the environment."

My work focuses on measuring environmental policy stringency, analyzing the impact of extreme climate events, and understanding the effects of financial institutions' communication on markets. I combine advanced econometric methods with innovative text mining and machine learning techniques (Python, R).
