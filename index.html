Introduction

I am an Assistant Professor in Economics at the FGES, Université Catholique de Lille, and a member of the Laboratoire Interdisciplinaire des Transitions de Lille (LITL). My research operates at the intersection of environmental economics, political economy, and finance, focusing broadly on the evaluation of public policies, the macroeconomic impacts of climate risks, and the analysis of international financial markets.

My methodological approach leverages rigorous econometric modeling with advanced text mining and machine learning techniques. Proficient in Python, R, and Stata, I specialize in harnessing novel data sources, ranging from large unstructured textual corpora to geospatial meteorological datasets to explore climate-related economic risks (phisical and transition risks) and policies impact economic development and sustainability.
