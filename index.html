Introduction

I am an Assistant Professor in Economics at the FGES — Université Catholique de Lille and a researcher at the Laboratoire Interdisciplinaire des Transitions de Lille (LITL). My research operates at the intersection of environmental economics, political economy, and finance, focusing on the evaluation of environmental policies, the macroeconomic impacts of climate risks, and the analysis of international financial markets.

My methodological approach combines rigorous econometric modelling with advanced text mining and machine learning techniques. Proficient in Python, R, and Stata, I specialize in harnessing novel data sources — from large unstructured textual corpora to geospatial meteorological datasets — to study how climate-related risks (physical and transition) and public policies shape economic development and sustainability.

Research Interests
